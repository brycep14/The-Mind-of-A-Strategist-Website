## Artist Statement Page

File: `artist-statement.html`

### Browser/Page Title

- Artist Statement | Bryce Peterson

### Intro

- Why the gallery exists
- A brief statement on process, philosophy, and the kind of intellectual atmosphere I want the work to create.

### Body

- I’m Bryce Peterson, a strategist and storyteller interested in the ideas beneath behavior, culture, and brand meaning. I like work that feels composed but still alive, where clarity does not flatten feeling.

### Philosophy

- Strategy should feel like a lens, not a lecture. The best ideas name a tension people already feel and then offer a more resonant way of seeing the world through language, design, and narrative shape.

### Vision

- Long term, I want to make work that holds together as both thought and atmosphere: ideas with cultural sensitivity, editorial elegance, and enough staying power to feel worth preserving.

### Title Wall

- Title Wall
- How do we make complex finance feel human enough to trust?
- Northline | 2025 | Morning Study

### Context

- A growing financial brand needed to speak to ambitious young professionals who felt underserved by sterile, jargon-heavy messaging. Expertise was plentiful in the category. Emotional clarity was not.

### Key Research

- Add the most important audience, category, interview, or cultural findings here. This section can hold the patterns you noticed before the final insight came into focus.

### Insight

- People do not want to feel managed by their money. They want to feel understood while they are still becoming who they hope to be.

### Idea

- We reframed the brand from a distant authority into a composed strategic partner, turning complexity into calm and replacing performance language with confidence-building language.

### Execution

- Study 01: Editorial landing pages with warmer pacing and more breathable information hierarchy.
- Study 02: A messaging system that translated technical products into plainspoken reassurance.
- Study 03: A visual direction that used softness, space, and restraint to make trust feel ambient.

### Reflection

- Trust is rarely built by information alone. It emerges when a brand reduces loneliness around an important decision.

- Title Wall
- How do we help a heritage brand matter to a new generation?
- Aster & Co. | 2024 | Archive Light

### Context

- A respected legacy brand had recognition, but younger audiences experienced it as fixed in the past. Awareness existed. Relevance did not.

### Key Research

- Add the most important audience, category, interview, or cultural findings here. This section can hold the patterns you noticed before the final insight came into focus.

### Insight

- People do not reject history. They reject history that arrives without a living point of view.

### Idea

- We treated the archive as a source of contemporary energy, using heritage not as proof of prestige but as material for a more expressive and modern identity.

### Execution

- Study 01: Campaign narratives built around reinterpretation rather than commemoration.
- Study 02: A styling and content system that made the archive feel participatory, not precious.
- Study 03: Launch touchpoints that connected craftsmanship to present-day self-expression.

### Reflection

- The past becomes powerful when it creates permission for something new rather than asking to be admired on its own.
